# AI, Art and a Search for Purpose
Every time I create anything now, I feel like I'm wasting my time. I can choose months of having fun and researching the topic at hand, or choose a week of excruciating yelling at a very apologetic chatbot. 

## Portfolio Mania
As a comp sci student before ChatGPT hit the market, the number one advice given out to get a job was to keep an **active portfolio**. Create games, websites or anything with code all to show that you love coding, and you enjoy doing it in your free time, something recruiters like. This caused everyone to create the bare minimum, and a lot of it as well, just to show that you can create anything at all.

When ChatGPT released this problem became even bigger. Now you have over 100 applicants all having the same portfolio, where all the apps look superb and modern and ready for deployment, but none of these people actually like coding. They just want the job, which they got by prompting their way to it. Can I complain about this? Here the end result speaks for itself. Someone created a good enough portfolio to be recognized by a recruiter, and a person can now sustain themselves. New technology to create even faster than we have before, which is good, right? 

We're taught to be successful, you just need to have a bunch of shit there. showing it off.
